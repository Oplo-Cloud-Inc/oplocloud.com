The mission

Most technology is built to capture attention. Ours is built to give it back.

Oplo exists because the tools people rely on have drifted away from the people who rely on them. We make the whole stack — silicon, devices, operating system, cloud — so that when something matters, one company is accountable for all of it.

We are independent and patient, and we take no advertising revenue. Our products answer only to the people who buy them — whether that's one person, a company of ten thousand, or a country of millions.

Principles

Three promises we design against.

Privacy is the default.

Data stays on the device unless you decide otherwise. We sell products, not audiences, so there is nothing to mine and no one to sell it to.

Repairable by design.

Every device ships with published schematics, ten years of parts, and screws instead of glue. If you own it, you can open it.

Accessible to every ability.

Voice, switch, and screen-reader control are built in the first week of a product's life, not bolted on in the last.

The platform

One stack, from silicon to service.

Every layer is ours, so every layer is accountable. When something breaks at 3 a.m., there is no vendor to wait on.

Oplo Silicon O-series chips designed in-house, tuned for on-device intelligence and a decade of updates. Layer — silicon
Devices Halo and Slate. Repairable by design, with parts and schematics published on day one. Layer — hardware
Oplo OS One operating system across every device, small enough to audit and stable enough to certify. Layer — software
Oplo Cloud Compute and storage in your region or your building — the same APIs either way. Layer — cloud
Services Deployment, training, and 24/7 support from engineers who ship the product, not scripts. Layer — people
